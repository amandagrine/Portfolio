Redesigning an Album cover
I redesigned the cover of David Bowie’s album "Reality", designed by Jonathan Barnbrook and Rex Ray. The songs on this album follow the theme of a shifting and abstract reality, which has been creatively illustrated on the original cover of the album, depicting David Bowie as a character from an animation. Although I find the overall composition of the album unappealing, which is why I wanted to redesign it, the concept of portraying the theme of a fake and incomprehensible reality as an animation is very clever, therefore I kept this idea when creating my original design of the album cover.

This album is also deeply connected to New York City because it was influenced by Bowie’s life there, so I decided to depict a scenic view of New York on the cover. To illustrate the main focus of the album – reality being abstract with Bowie trying to find his place in it – I illustrated Bowie, as an animated character, falling out of the city, therefore dramatizing the concept of the album even more.

The cover was redesigned in Affinity.
